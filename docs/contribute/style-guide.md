# Style guide

## Voice

Write like a generous colleague who has already made the mistakes and is saving someone else the bruises.

## Titles

Use practical questions:

- Good: **How do I OCR a batch of scanned PDFs?**
- Less good: **Tesseract tutorial**

## Scope

One page should solve one problem. If the workflow needs three separate tools and two philosophical caveats, split it.

## Evidence and testing

Say what was tested. Say what was not tested. A small verified example is worth more than a grand untested claim.

## Ethics and rights

Mention ethics whenever the workflow touches:

- living people;
- unpublished or personal work;
- oral histories;
- copyrighted text, subtitles, or images;
- family history or sensitive records;
- platform data;
- AI-generated claims.

## Common traps should be specific

Bad: “Be careful.”  
Good: “Do not remove negation words if you plan sentiment analysis.”

## Accessibility

Prefer readable alt text, clear captions, and formats that do not require expensive proprietary tools unless the page is specifically about that tool.
