# How do I visualise a frequency list with Voyant?

<div class="answer-meta" markdown>
<span>Visualization</span>
<span>beginner</span>
<span>15–30 min</span>
</div>

## What you are trying to do

You need a quick exploratory view of frequent words or document-level patterns.

!!! quote "One-sentence version"
    Start with a tiny sample, keep provenance, and only scale up once the workflow survives testing.

## You need

- Plain-text corpus or pasted text

## Tools

- [Voyant Tools](https://voyant-tools.org)

## Workflow

1. Upload the corpus to Voyant.
2. Inspect Cirrus, Trends, Contexts, and Summary.
3. Edit stop-word settings.
4. Export images or links for reporting.
5. Write a short interpretation that does not overclaim.

## Output

Interactive visualization or exported image.

## Check yourself

- Did stop words change the story?
- Are the most visible words actually meaningful?
- Can you explain what the visualization does not show?

## Common traps

- Treating a word cloud as analysis.
- Ignoring corpus composition.
- Using default stop words uncritically.

## Practice task

Students produce a “bad word cloud” and then repair it with better preprocessing and interpretation.

## Useful extension

Turn the workflow into a small reusable checklist: input, command or tool action, output, quality check, and known failure mode.
