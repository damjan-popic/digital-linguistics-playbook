# Ethics checklist

Use this before publishing, scraping, archiving, mapping, or using AI on cultural material.

## People

- Are living people represented directly or indirectly?
- Could the data reveal family, health, political, religious, migration, or identity information?
- Is consent needed?
- Is anonymisation possible and meaningful?

## Rights

- Who owns the text, image, audio, video, or dataset?
- What does the license allow?
- Are you allowed to redistribute the data, or only analyze it privately?
- Are platform terms relevant?

## Communities

- Does the material belong to a community with expectations about access?
- Is there cultural sensitivity around the source?
- Would public release create extraction without benefit?

## AI

- Are AI-generated claims checked against sources?
- Could generated output expose private data?
- Are users told when AI use is allowed, required, or forbidden?

## Publication

- Is there a contact or takedown route?
- Are limitations visible?
- Is restricted access more appropriate than public access?
