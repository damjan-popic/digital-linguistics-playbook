# How do I harvest bilingual subtitles responsibly?

<div class="answer-meta" markdown>
<span>Subtitling</span>

</div>

## What you are trying to do

You want colloquial parallel lines from subtitle data while staying alert to rights and quality.

!!! quote "One-sentence version"
    Start with a tiny sample, keep provenance, and only scale up once the workflow survives testing.

## You need

- Movie or subtitle IDs
- Language pair
- Collection policy

## Tools

- [OpenSubtitles API](https://opensubtitles.stoplight.io/)
- [pysrt](https://github.com/byroot/pysrt)

## Workflow

1. Check API terms and copyright context.
2. Download only what you are allowed to use.
3. Align subtitle timelines cautiously.
4. Filter by reading speed, timing overlap, and language ID.
5. Store metadata and avoid sharing restricted text.

## Output

Subtitle metadata and aligned lines where permitted.

## Check yourself

- Are rights and reuse conditions clear?
- Are fansub quality differences documented?
- Does timing alignment correspond to translation?

## Common traps

- Treating subtitles as clean translations.
- Publishing copyrighted subtitles openly.
- Ignoring censorship, condensation, and adaptation.

## Classroom version

Turn this into an ethics case study: students design a reuse policy before touching data.

## Useful extension

Turn the workflow into a small reusable checklist: input, command or tool action, output, quality check, and known failure mode.
